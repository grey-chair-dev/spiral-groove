 Business Overview
Spiral Groove Records is an independent record shop and community hub specializing in new and used vinyl, tapes/CDs, and select audio gear. Based at 215 Main St, Milford, OH, it serves local shoppers across Milford, Clermont County, and the Greater Cincinnati area, with nationwide reach via online sales. Beyond retail, the shop hosts events and aims to grow an “event space” offering (past/future shows, inquiries, FAQs). Brand tone is warm, nostalgic, and analog-friendly—like chatting with your favorite record shop owner.

Core offerings

New & used vinyl (LPs/singles), CDs, cassettes

Audio equipment (turntables, amps, accessories)

Vinyl crafts/merch (e.g., bowls, décor)

Buying/trade-in for collections

Events and emerging basement event space

Service area

Local: Milford & surrounding Clermont County

Regional: Greater Cincinnati

National: Web store (inventory synced from Square)

Unique Value Proposition (UVP)
Authentic analog experience: Curated vinyl selection with knowledgeable staff; “crate-digging” discovery.

Community-first: In-store events and evolving event space strengthen local music culture.

Trustworthy inventory: Commitment to accurate, real-time stock levels by integrating Square POS directly into the website (solves current mismatch pain point).

Approachable curation: Simple, nostalgic brand voice and clean UI that’s easy for casual shoppers and serious collectors alike.

Market Analysis (Competitive Landscape & Opportunities)
Landscape (high level)

Regional record shops compete on curation, staff knowledge, and event/community presence.

Online marketplaces (Discogs, eBay) compete on breadth and price transparency but lack local community feel.

Big-box/streaming compete for casual listeners; Spiral Groove can win on tactile experience and staff guidance.

Key pain points & opportunities

Inventory mismatch online vs. in-store: Current Square-to-website desync erodes trust and conversion; fixing it is high-impact.

Discovery & new arrivals: Collectors want frequent updates and “what just dropped” visibility.

Events as a growth lever: Dedicated event space page, calendar, inquiries, and media elevate brand and repeat visits.

Local partnerships: Cross-promotions with cafés, bars, venues, and festivals amplify reach and SEO (backlinks, citations).

Simpler UX: Minimalist, fast site with clear paths to “Shop,” “New Arrivals,” and “Visit/Events” outperforms busy layouts.

Business Goals
Short-term (0–3 months)

GOAL ONE: Fix POS–website mismatch; make Square the single source of truth for web inventory & counts.

Launch New Arrivals and Coming Soon pages fed by Square categories/tags.

Implement simple, minimal design that feels “retro-modern” and fast.

Create Event Space one-pager: overview, capacity, tech specs, FAQ, inquiry form, and list past/upcoming shows.

Add About Us (history/origin), Where to Find Us (partners/events), and Sales & Promotions modules.

Mid-term (3–9 months)

Improve product data quality (pressing/label/condition standardized).

Add in-store pickup and local delivery options where feasible.

Build email list growth loops (signup on checkout, footer, and “New Arrivals Alerts”).

Pilot simple loyalty program (punch/stamp or Square loyalty).

Strengthen local partnerships and co-host events.

Long-term (9–18 months)

Expand event programming, merch line, and exclusive drops.

Launch limited online exclusives and seasonal promos.

Content engine (blog/spotlights: staff picks, local bands, collector guides) to lift SEO and brand authority.

Digital Growth Opportunities (Website & Marketing)
Inventory Integrity & UX (highest impact)

Direct Square integration (catalog + stock counts + modifiers) to ensure the site reflects what’s actually in stock.

Enforce canonical product identifiers (barcode/label/pressing) and clear condition grading (M/NM/VG+…).

New Arrivals auto-collections (last 7/14/30 days), Coming Soon (pre-announced stock), Recently Restocked.

Collection filters: Genre • Condition • Price • New/Used • Label • Decade • Staff Picks.

Event Space Module

Dedicated subpage: overview, capacity, photos, calendar embeds, rental form, FAQs, rate ranges, tech specs.

“Past & Upcoming” section to build credibility.

Add structured data (Event schema) for SEO where applicable.

Content & Community

“About Me/Us” story page with archival photos for authenticity.

Blog/Notes: staff picks, “Vinyl 101,” “How to choose a turntable,” local band spotlights.

“Places to Find Us” hub: partnerships, pop-ups, markets, festivals; link out to social channels.

Sales & Promotions

Seasonal campaigns (Record Store Day, holidays).

Email list-building with incentives (“10% off your first order” or “Early access to New Arrivals”).

Lightweight loyalty via Square; feature rewards progress in account area.

Performance & Trust

Fast, accessible frontend (clean, retro-inspired UI, no clutter).

Prominent store policies (returns on used/new, grading standards, shipping/packaging).

Reviews & testimonials; UGC via Instagram/TikTok embeds.

Measurement

KPIs: Inventory sync accuracy %, New Arrivals conversion rate, Email list growth, Repeat purchase rate, Event inquiries submitted, Average order value, % BOPIS (buy online, pickup in store).
